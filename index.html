About

I am a 17-year-old student from Ahmedabad, India. My interest is in the questions that sit at the edge of what science can currently answer — why anything feels like something, how the brain generates presence from absence, and what consciousness actually is beneath the explanations that keep missing it.

I think at a systems level. I identify gaps by instinct, build frameworks independently, and then look for the science that confirms or breaks them. The sequence — curiosity first, proof later — is not a limitation. It is how most real questions begin.

My long-term goal is neuroscience research focused on consciousness. Everything else is infrastructure toward that.

Scientific Hypothesis

Neurological and Thermodynamic Mechanisms Underlying Anomalous Presence Perception

A unified scientific explanation for the universal human experience of sensing a deceased person's presence. Proposes four interacting mechanisms: infrasound-induced cortical arousal, predictive processing failure, Neural Persistence, and Cardiac Neural Persistence. Now at version 16. Essay

Consciousness? — The Accident and Its Cost

A philosophical essay examining consciousness not as an evolutionary achievement but as an unresolved consequence. Traces the emergence of self-awareness, its external and internal costs, the illusion of a special self it constructs, and the paradox of a question that cannot be answered by the thing asking it. Closes on the hard problem — and a direction worth pursuing.
